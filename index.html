What is Classical Music?

The term "classical music" covers roughly 1,000 years of Western art music — from medieval monks singing plainchant in stone churches to composers today working with electronics, mathematics, and silence itself. It is not one thing. It is eight distinct eras, each with its own aesthetic, its own rules, and its own revolution.

Eight Eras

Each era was a revolution — a rejection of what came before and an invention of something new.

1000 – 1400

Early Music

Plainchant, polyphony, and the birth of notation. The Church shapes everything.

Hildegard · Machaut · Léonin
1400 – 1600

Renaissance

Humanism arrives. Polyphony reaches its peak. Opera is invented.

Palestrina · Tallis · Byrd
1600 – 1750

Baroque

Drama, ornament, and the basso continuo. Bach and Handel at the summit.

Bach · Handel · Vivaldi
1750 – 1820

Classical Era

Clarity, balance, and form. The symphony and string quartet perfected.

Mozart · Haydn · Beethoven
1810 – 1920

Romantic Era

Emotion unleashed. Orchestras swell. Wagner reinvents opera.

Chopin · Wagner · Tchaikovsky
1830 – 1920

Nationalism

Folk music becomes art music. Identity becomes political.

Dvořák · Grieg · Sibelius
1900 – 1950

Modern Era

Tonality shatters. Stravinsky riots. Schoenberg invents the 12-tone system.

Debussy · Stravinsky · Bartók
1950 – present

Contemporary

Silence is music. Minimalism. Electronics. Everything is possible.

Cage · Glass · Reich
